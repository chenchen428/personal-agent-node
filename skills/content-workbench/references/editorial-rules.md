# Editorial Rules

## Preserve

- factual meaning, uncertainty, and responsibility;
- names, dates, measurements, links, quotes, code, and commands;
- the author's intended register unless a new audience was specified;
- important caveats and counterarguments.

## Improve

- one clear idea per paragraph;
- descriptive headings that match the section below;
- parallel structure in lists;
- tables only for repeated-field comparison;
- concise summaries that do not introduce new claims;
- emphasis on the smallest phrase that carries the point.

## Avoid

- generic opening throat-clearing;
- fabricated transitions, anecdotes, statistics, or quotes;
- headings for every paragraph;
- decorative blockquotes or bolding entire paragraphs;
- changing a recommendation's strength;
- flattening disagreement into false consensus.

When the source is poorly structured, save an edited copy and briefly state the structural changes rather than pretending the new structure was original.
