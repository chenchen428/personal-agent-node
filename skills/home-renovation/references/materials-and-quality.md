# Materials And Quality Decisions

## Start From Performance

Define the requirement before choosing a brand, color, or trend:

- location and exposure to water, heat, sunlight, abrasion, impact, chemicals, stains, pets, or children;
- substrate, edge, joint, transition, fixing, sealant, movement, drainage, ventilation, and cleaning conditions;
- appearance target, allowed variation, aging behavior, repairability, replaceability, maintenance skill, and expected service life;
- relevant test report, declaration, certification, installation instruction, warranty, and local acceptance requirement.

Verify current manufacturer documents for the exact product and region. A family name, showroom sample, marketplace listing, or influencer post is not an exact specification.

## Comparison Matrix

Use consistent fields:

```text
candidate / intended location / exact identifier / performance evidence /
substrate and installation prerequisites / edge-joint details / sample status /
cleaning and maintenance / repair path / availability / lead time / warranty /
price boundary / unresolved risk
```

Separate inherent material properties from system performance. Waterproofing, flooring, paint, tile, stone, cabinetry, counters, windows, doors, acoustic treatment, and lighting depend on preparation and installation as well as the visible product.

## Samples And Mockups

Approve physical samples under representative daylight and artificial light. Record supplier, product, batch or lot when relevant, finish, date, location, and approval status.

Require a mockup when several interfaces or subjective outcomes meet, such as:

- tile layout, grout, trim, niche, slope, and sealant;
- wood tone, veneer matching, edge detail, hardware, and lighting;
- stone pattern, seam, edge, backsplash, sink, and support;
- wall color, sheen, substrate preparation, grazing light, and adjacent finishes;
- curtain fullness, track, pocket, blackout, and opening clearance.

The approved sample and mockup become acceptance evidence. Photograph them with neutral lighting and a stable label; do not rely on chat thumbnails alone.

## Substitutions

Treat every substitution as a new decision. Compare exact performance, appearance, dimensions, interfaces, installation method, certifications, warranty, lead time, price credit, and downstream changes. “Equivalent” requires evidence.

Reject an undocumented substitution when it changes concealed work, safety, fit, visible matching, maintenance, warranty, or future replacement.

## Inspection Hold Points

Create hold points before work becomes concealed or irreversible. Depending on scope, these may include substrate preparation, service routes, pressure or functional tests, waterproofing and drainage evidence, fire stopping, fixing support, dimensions for fabricated items, finish mockups, and commissioning.

Use current local requirements and the responsible professional's inspection plan. Do not invent a universal test method, duration, thickness, spacing, or pass value.

## Handover Evidence

Collect only applicable items:

- approved drawings, revisions, and as-built locations;
- product schedule, exact finishes, spare materials, batch information, and care instructions;
- test and commissioning records;
- warranties, invoices, installer contacts, and claim conditions;
- shutoff, isolation, access-panel, filter, drain, and maintenance locations;
- open defects, responsible party, target date, and closeout evidence.
