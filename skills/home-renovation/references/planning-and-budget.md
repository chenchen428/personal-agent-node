# Planning, Budget, And Quotations

## Scope Breakdown

Break the project into comparable packages:

1. survey, design, engineering, approvals, and management;
2. protection, temporary works, demolition, removal, and disposal;
3. structure and building-envelope repair where applicable;
4. plumbing, drainage, electrical, gas, HVAC, ventilation, network, and controls;
5. partitions, doors, windows, ceilings, floors, walls, waterproofing, and finishes;
6. cabinetry, counters, sanitaryware, lighting, appliances, hardware, and fittings;
7. furniture, curtains, rugs, artwork, plants, styling, moving, cleaning, and temporary housing;
8. testing, commissioning, defects, warranties, tax, fees, and contingency.

Use only relevant packages. Confirm whether owner-supplied items include delivery, storage, installation, accessories, consumables, commissioning, and warranty coordination.

## Budget Model

Show:

```text
Base committed scope
+ priced but uncommitted choices
+ risk allowance by named uncertainty
+ optional upgrades
+ owner-side costs
= forecast total
```

Do not prescribe one universal contingency percentage. Size each allowance from project stage, survey completeness, building age, concealed-condition exposure, design maturity, price validity, and contract risk. A risk allowance is not spare money for scope growth.

Update the forecast after design freeze, opening-up work, coordinated measurements, quotation return, and every approved change.

## Like-For-Like Quotation Table

Normalize every bidder or supplier against:

```text
scope item / drawing or specification / quantity / unit / material and grade /
brand-model or approved equal / preparation / labor / accessories / protection /
testing / waste / tax / lead time / warranty / exclusions / assumptions
```

Flag:

- missing quantities or units;
- allowances presented as fixed prices;
- vague “standard material” descriptions;
- unusually low items with missing preparation or accessories;
- duplicated work across packages;
- owner-supplied items with no installation responsibility;
- payment that gets ahead of verified work;
- change-order pricing or acceptance rules that are absent.

Total price is not comparable until scope is comparable.

## Dependency Schedule

Plan backwards from required completion and record dependencies rather than promising an unsupported duration.

Typical logic:

```text
survey -> brief -> concept -> coordinated measurement -> technical approval
-> scope/specification -> comparable quotes -> contract -> long-lead release
-> site preparation -> concealed work -> hold-point inspection -> close-up
-> finishes -> measured joinery/stone/glass -> fixtures -> commissioning
-> defects -> cleaning -> handover
```

Identify items that must wait for site measurement after finishes or substrates are ready. Keep float for approvals, samples, failed inspections, drying/curing conditions, delivery damage, and defect correction.

## Change Control

Before authorizing a change, record:

- reason and evidence;
- exact scope removed and added;
- price and tax;
- schedule and downstream interfaces;
- drawing/specification revision;
- warranty or compliance impact;
- approval owner and date.

Verbal site changes are unresolved changes, not accepted scope.
